Earnings Reports

Earnings reports are documents released by companies, usually on a quarterly schedule. They detail the company's earnings, spending, and overall economic shifts.


Earnings reports allow investors to stay up-to-date on a company's financial situation. Therefore it is important for investors to look at and understand a company's earning report before investing in them, as the report is usually a telltale sign of whether or not a company is profitable.


They aren't just guiding posts though, earning reports can heavily affect a stock price. If a company reports earnings below the expected amount, their price will most likely shoot down, but the contrary is also true.

Above is SNAP's chart after reporting earnings that far exceeded what investors thought as going to be reported. What we see is a large jump in volume and jump in stock price as investors realize there is more value in the company than previously thought. The jump was overnight, and was drastic, but unpredictable.


While it is possible to estimate earnings before they are announced, one can never know what will actually happen. This makes earnings reports popular for day traders, as the volatility attracts them.


Earnings reports can be a good thing, but it is important to understand the impact they can have on one's investment whether good or bad.
