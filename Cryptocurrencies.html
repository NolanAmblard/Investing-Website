Cryptocurrencies


What are cryptocurrencies?

Cryptocurrencies are digital currencies in which "transactions are verified and records maintained by a decentralized system using cryptography, rather than by a centralized authority."

Pretty much, they are currencies that don't take a physical form (they don't have coins or bills). They are traded online and only a fixed amount exists in the world.

For example, the most popular currency, Bitcoin, only has 21 million bitcoins that can be mined (meaning only 21 million bitcoins can be in circulation). This limits inflation and gives Bitcoin and actual value.


How can I make money from cryptocurrencies?

Similar to forex trading, trading crypto involves investing in a currency with the hopes it will increase. Also similar to forex, this is risky as it is very hard to predict the future of crytpo.

Unlike crypto, though, there are less factors that can affect the price of crypto, making it slightly more predictable.



Above is the all time graph for Bitcoin. Bitcoin was heavily talked about at the end of 2017, hence why it blew up to such proportions. However, as hype around it died down, it lost a lot of value. There in lies the issue with cryptocurrencies. Since they are not the official currency for any territory, if everyone quits using or caring about them, they become worthless. And, unlike a most currencies, they aren't backed by any commodity like gold so there is no safety net when it comes to crypto.
