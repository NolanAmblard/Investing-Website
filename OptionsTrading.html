Options Trading

Options are a contract that a linked with 100 shares of an underlying stock. In their most simple use, options include calls and puts. While there are many other strategies involving options, they all revolve around buying and selling the two basic contracts. Options can be used to hedge a current position or they can be used to profit off of movements in an underlying stock. Options have 3 things that determine their value: expiration date, strike price, and volatility.

Calls

A call is where an investor buys a contract that lets the buyer purchase 100 shares of contract at a preditermined price. Buying calls is a very bullish action.

Puts

A put is where an investor buys a contract that lets the buyer sell 100 shares of contract at a preditermined price. Buying puts is a very bearish action.
