401k

A 401k is a retirement plan offered by some US companies to their employees. Employees can invest a percentage of their pre-tax income into previously selected mutual funds, and the employer matches the employee contribution up to a certain percent (typically 6%).


Starting at 56.5 years old or when a person retires, people can withdraw money from their 401k investment account. Taxes need to be paid on the amount withdrawn and the amount withdrawn is considered the person's personal income (this decides tax brackets).
