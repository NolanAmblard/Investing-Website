Spreads

Buying a spread involves buying and selling calls or puts on a company that have the same expiration date.


This therefore means that there are 4 types of spreads: bullish call spread, bearish call spread, bullish put spread, bearish put spread.

The two most simple are a bullish call spread and a bearish put spread. A bullish call spread involves buying a call and selling call options (buy the more expensive one and sell the cheaper one. The same goes for a bearish put spread.


Why buy spreads?

For one, spreads allow you to get closer to in the money for the same price, therefore reducing risk significantly. They also have much less volatitlity and therefore don't shift as much with price changes. However, the downside of spreads is that profit is capped and that there is much less volume (less people look to buy and sell spreads making it harder to get in and out of an investment).
