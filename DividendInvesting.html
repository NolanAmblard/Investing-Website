Dividend Investing

Dividend Investing is similar to long term investing, but focuses on accumulating passive income. Dividends are payments made out to investors every once in a while from the profits a company made. They are measured in comparison to a ticker's share price. Since they are paid out to an investors account, they can either be kept as money or reinvested so the investor can make more next time. Dividend investors tend to have pretty diversified portfolios as dividends can change at any time and these investors dont want to be stuck holding dead weight. When there is an economic downturn, these investors tend to get hurt pretty bad as a lot of companies cut their dividends in order to keep cash and not go bankrupt.
