Bull Market

A bullish market is a term that describes a market that increases with time. A bullish market is often compared to the growth of the S&P, so when the S&P is growing year over year, we are in a bull market. Bull markets are usually characterized by stocks hitting all time highs and unemployment being low. Bull markets are more common than bear markets, hence why the economy is known to grow over time. This is also why long-term investing is so effective. Even if and index goes down on the short-term, chances are it will rise in the long-term.
