Options

Options are a contract that a linked with 100 shares of an underlying stock. In their most simple use, options include calls and puts. While there are many other strategies involving options, they all revolve around buying and selling the two basic contracts. Options, by nature, are extremely risky as for every $1 move in the underlying stock, an options intrinsic value moves by $100 (if the option is in the money). Options can be used to hedge a current position or they can be used to profit off of movements in an underlying stock. Options have 3 things that determine their value: expiration date, strike price, and volatility. Options can be bought or sold, both. Selling an option contract does the opposite of what buying one does (buying a call is bullish while selling one is bearish).
