IPOs

An IPO is an intial public offering, meaning a company is made public and shares of that company are able to be bought and sold.

IPOs tend to be very volatile, as not many people know their true value. This can lead to IPOs shooting up in the weeks/months after release or crashing down.

For example, in 2015, Fitbit was the IPO that everyone was watching. Their watches were going viral and people were excited to be able to invest in a company.



As shown in Fitbit's all time chart, it opened at 20.00 and quickly rose to the high 40s. Soon after tho, the hype died down and stock declined rapidly as people realized that the company wasn't worth as much as they once thought. Now, the stock has stabilized around the 6.50 mark, where it doesn't move much.
